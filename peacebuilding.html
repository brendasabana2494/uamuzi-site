YOUTH IN PEACEBUILDING

The inclusion of young people in Peacebuilding processes is bound to facilitate sustainable peace in a society, by redirecting their energies to the implementation of constructive peace projects.

Incorporation and utilization of youth in peacebuilding processes will facilitate their transformation from agents of violent conflict, to agents of peace in their societies. The youth are more likely to avoid violence and take part in peacebuilding if they are granted opportunities to engage in political participation by forging connections between them and their representatives, building constituencies for peace, and educate them by building their confidence and self-esteem.

PEACEBUILDING

Uamuzi is a people-driven peacebuilding platform aimed at empowering young people to take part in preventing electoral violence and promoting peaceful coexistence among communities in Kenya. Through our special feature Mulika which is a Swahili word that translates to gleam/light up, the youth will be able to report on violence and other incidents which may occur before, during or after elections in real time. Our intent is to not only deliver an early warning about a specific, geographically-constrained violence risk, but primarily to empower people to take action and to initiate mitigation measures before a catastrophic event occurs.

The involvement of the youth in peacebuilding relies on the direct participation of those most likely to be affected. Without the involvement of stakeholders and those at risk, interventions and responses to incidents are likely to be inadequate. Consideration of Gender perspectives and People living with disability is essential to recognize that different groups have different vulnerabilities according to culture, gender, location, religion or other characteristics that influence their capacity to effectively prepare for, prevent and respond. Women and men often play different roles in society and have different access to information in disaster situations. The elderly and socio-economically disadvantaged are also vulnerable. Uamuzi has been tailored to meet the needs of every group in every vulnerable community.

  • Reporting

    Through the Mulika feature on the platform, the youth will be able to report on any kind of violence being witnessed pre, during and post elections. Upon accessing the feature, they will be required to provide the exact incident being faced, the location where it is happening and the level of threat being witnessed.

    This allows them to waste little or no time in reporting and allowing for quick response from the relevant authorities. The data once received will be sent to a special section on our backend for analysis of the situation.

  • Analytics and Monitoring

    The data collected will help to understand the exact risks affecting the youth and their communities at large, including hazards, exposure, vulnerabilities, and coping capacities. The platform will use Artificial Intelligence (AI) to analyze the data collected and will categorize them based on age, gender and region. This information will help during mapping of the affected regions, gain a better understanding of the roots causes and dynamics, detect threat narratives and disinformation campaigns while understanding the main topics of discussions.

  • Civic Education and Awareness

    Uamuzi is considered essential as a peacebuilding platform due to its ability of informing the youth to respond correctly to information. Using the analytics derived from the collected data, the platform will be used to conduct civic education programs that employ youth-friendly and easy to understand tactics that will reach the grassroots levels, including rural and remote locations. In collaboration with like-minded stakeholders, awareness creation will be disseminated through posters, video, audios, customized messaging, and blogging.

    They will be disseminated as Direct Messages or as sponsored posts and will have priority over the regular posts, thus reaching the youth over a period of time.
