About Volunteering
Committed to generating, disseminating, and preserving knowledge, and to working with others to bring to existence some of the best innovations.

Uamuzi Hub is an engineering group with fresh insights, new ideas, and diverse skills learning and working collaboratively and strategically to strengthen our community. The Hub is committed to generating, disseminating, and preserving knowledge, and to working with others to bring to existence some of the best innovations.

We are primarily focused on young Engineers aged below 35 years who are selected through online applications, interviews and boot camps. We accept requests on a rolling basis.

Intensely curious and driven to explore, our Engineers value rigorous analytical thinking, ingenuity, hands-on problem solving and big new ideas. Stimulating, supportive, and playful, our Hub is a second home for many engineers.

Uamuzi Foundation Volunteers also known as UFV is a powerful means of engaging young people in tackling development challenges and transform the pace and nature of development.

We benefit both the society at large and the individual by strengthening trust, solidarity and reciprocity among the youth and creating opportunities for participation.

We offer vital help to people in need, worthwhile causes, and the community, but the benefits can be even greater for you, the volunteer. The right match can help you find friends, connect with the community, learn new skills and even advance your career. We work with volunteers who are qualified and highly motivated in developing programs and promoting the value and recognition of volunteerism.

Our Volunteers

  • Provide technical support and enhance capacity in our areas of focus such as voter education, policy and advocacy, youth engagement, disability inclusion, transparency and accountability, youth leadership, gender equality and environmental conservation.
  • Facilitate new spaces for dialogue and action by reaching out to people including those marginalized by bringing their voices and knowledge into collective action that is crucial in building ownership.
  • Raise awareness to champion changes and inspire others to facilitate durable changes in mindsets and behaviors in our communities.
  • Measure progress on our projects through collecting and analyzing data, providing expertise and support participatory forms of planning, monitoring and evaluation.

Social cohesion and trust are strengthened through individual and collective volunteer action which leads to sustainable outcomes for people, by people.

Eligibility

  • Should be below the age of 35 years.
  • Should read, write and communicate effectively in one or more local languages.
  • Should have a strong community presence, local knowledge and networks.
  • Should demonstrate a strong commitment to the values and principles of volunteering with respect for diversity.
  • Should be interested and committed to the values and principles of volunteering.
  • Higher technical diploma, Bachelor’s or Master’s degree is an added advantage.
